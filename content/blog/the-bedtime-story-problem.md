Hello, friends. Gus Gander here — your friendly neighborhood storyteller and proud member of the Silly Goose Tales flock.

Let me tell you about a moment every parent knows too well. It's 8pm. The teeth are brushed. The pajamas are on (mostly). Your little one is tucked in bed, eyes bright with expectation, and they say those four magical words: "Tell me a story."

And your brain? Completely empty. Not a single plot point. Not even a "once upon a time" to get you started.

You've been a superhero all day — chef, chauffeur, mediator, detective (where DID that other shoe go?), and now you're expected to conjure up an enchanting tale featuring... let me check my notes... a dinosaur who's also a detective and solves mysteries about missing socks. Right. No problem.

This is what I call The Bedtime Story Problem, and it's the reason Silly Goose Tales exists.

Here's the thing: your child doesn't need another screen before bed. They don't need an app with seventeen buttons and in-app purchases. They need a story. A real one. With their name in it. One that meets them exactly where they are — whether they're feeling giggly, sleepy, brave, or a little bit worried about tomorrow.

That's what we do. Every single night, a new story lands in your inbox. You open it on your phone, you read it together, and boom — you're the hero who delivered exactly the right tale at exactly the right moment.

No login. No scrolling. No "Mom, can we watch just one more?" Just a story, ready when you are.

In this blog, I'll be sharing thoughts about bedtime, storytelling, the wild world of parenting, and occasionally some behind-the-scenes peeks at how we craft these nightly tales. Think of it as storytime for the grownups.

Welcome to the flock. We're glad you're here.
